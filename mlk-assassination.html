Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. was assassinated on April 4, 1968, in Memphis. His death triggered national grief, uprisings, and a radical shift in the tone of the movement.

Image source: Assassin fells Martin Luther King Jr., April 4, 1968 - POLITICO.

The assassination of Dr. Martin Luther King, Jr. in April 1968 was a devastating moment in American history. His death not only plunged the nation into grief but also sparked widespread riots and a subsequent turmoil of the civil rights struggle. This act of targeted political violence served as both an endpoint for an era of hope and a beginning for a new, more radical phase of activism.

  • Type of Violence: Targeted political violence and state-enabled retaliation
  • Impact: Marked a tragic turning point that reshaped civil rights strategies
  • Memory: King's legacy endures as both a symbol of peaceful resistance and a martyr for justice

Connection to Oral Histories

Oral accounts describe the deep personal and communal loss experienced after his death, emphasizing how his death redefined the struggle for equality.
