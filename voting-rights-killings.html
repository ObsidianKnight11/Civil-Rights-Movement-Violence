Throughout the 1960s, Black citizens attempting to register to vote or exercise their political rights were met with lethal violence and intimidation. From beatings and shootings to bombings and arson, these acts were deliberate measures to disenfranchise Black voters and maintain racial hierarchies. The persistent threat created a climate of fear that activists had to overcome through immense personal sacrifice.

  • Type of Violence: Physical brutality and lethal intimidation
  • Impact: Highlighted systemic racial discrimination in political and social institutions
  • Memory: Is remembered as a painful but defining struggle for democratic participation

Connection to Oral Histories

Firsthand accounts describe personal experiences of arrest, beatings, and constant fear, illustrating how the fight for voting rights was intertwined with daily acts of violence.
