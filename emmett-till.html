Before tragedy struck, he was just a young boy, full of life and unaware of the storm that lay ahead. His image, a testament to the innocence lost to violence, remains a painful reminder of what should have been.

Image source: Biography of Emmett Till, Victim of Lynching.

In August 1955, 14-year-old Emmett Till was brutally murdered in Mississippi for allegedly offending a white woman. His mother, Mamie Till-Mobley, demanded an open-casket funeral that exposed the nation's brutality and ignited a wave of national outrage. This tragedy not only highlighted the extreme racism of the era but also became a powerful symbol of the need for change.

  • Type of Violence: Mob violence and racial terrorism
  • Impact: Spurred national awakening and galvanized the civil rights movement
  • Memory: His image and the open-casket funeral remain enduring symbols of injustice

Connection to Oral Histories

Activists recall Till's murder as a turning point that deepened their commitment to civil rights. Firsthand accounts reveal how this event catalyzed a collective memory of horror and resolve.
