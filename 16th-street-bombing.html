The 1963 bombing killed four girls attending Sunday school. The church had been a center of civil rights activism in Birmingham and was targeted by white supremacists.

Image source: The 16th Street Baptist Church Bombing And The Civil Rights Movement.

In September 1963, a bomb exploded at the 16th Street Baptist Church in Birmingham, Alabama, killing four young African American girls. This atrocity exposed the lethal nature of racial terrorism and served as a catalyst for civil rights reforms. The tragedy remains one of the most searing examples of hate-driven violence in American history.

  • Type of Violence: Hate crime and terrorism
  • Impact: Catalyzed public demands for racial justice and legislative reform
  • Memory: Continues to serve as a stark reminder of the cost of racial hatred

Connection to Oral Histories

Survivors and community members recount the bombing as a moment of collective grief and renewed determination to fight systemic oppression.
